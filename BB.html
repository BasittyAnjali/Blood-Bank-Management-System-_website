Who can give blood ?


Know Your Blood Group :-

The First step is to know your blood group and to which blood group your blood matches for donation.

A healthy Person :-

  • You should be fit and healthy.
  • You should be minimum weight over 7 stone 12 lbs or 50kg.
  • You are age between 17-66 (or 70 if you have given blood before).
  • You should not already be donated blood or have been treated for malaria within the last three months.

How often can I give blood?

  • Men can give blood every 12 weeks.
  • Women can give blood every 16 weeks.

How often can I give blood?

  • 15 minutes to donate.

Check you are able to give blood :-

If you have an existing medical condition or have a question about your eligibility to give blood you should check the health and travel section before you book on appointment.

Maintain your body before blood donation :-

  • Don't consume Alcohol or caffeine beverages before donation blood.
  • Have a healthy meal before your donation-avoid high fatty foods like humburgers, fries, or ice cream just before donation.
  • Maintain Hemoglobin level 12.5% minimum.

Maintain your body after blood donation :-

  • Keep eating iron-foods.
  • Drink an extra glasses of liquids and avoid alcohol over next 24 hours.
  • Keep the strip bandage on for the next several hours; to avoida skin rash, clean the area around the bandage with soap and water.
  • Don't do any heavy lifting or vigorous exercise for the rest of the day.
  • If the needle site starts to bleed, apply pressure and raise your arm straight up for 5-10 minutes or until bleeding stops.
  • If you experience dizziness or lightheadedness, stop what you’re doing and sit down or lie down until you feel better; avoid performing any activity where fainting may lead to injury for at least 24 hours.
  • If you donate frequently, be sure to take multivitamins with iron to ensure you continue to before your next donation.

To be careful :-

You should not be suffering from Cardiac arrest, hypertension, kidney alignments, epilepsy or diabetics. if so you must consult the doctor before donating the blood.

Ladies with a bad miscarriage should avoid donating blood for the next 6 months, or if you have been involved in some serious accident.
